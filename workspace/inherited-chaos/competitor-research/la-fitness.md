# Competitor Research: LA Fitness

## Overview
- **Locations:** 700+ clubs nationwide
- **Price Point:** $30-$45/month (similar to FitHub)
- **Brand:** Full-service gym with extensive amenities
- **Market Position:** Middle-tier, comprehensive facilities

## Their Rewards/App Features

### LA Fitness App
- **Digital check-in:** Contactless gym entry
- **Class schedules:** Browse and reserve spots
- **Workout plans:** Pre-built routines by goal
- **Trainer booking:** Schedule PT sessions
- **Amenity availability:** Pool/court availability status
- **Member referral program:** Bring friends, earn perks

### What Makes It Work
- **Utility first:** Solves practical problems (check-in, booking)
- **Comprehensive:** One app for all gym needs
- **Referral incentives:** Rewards that members actually want
- **Straightforward:** No gimmicks, just functionality

## Engagement Tactics
- Class reminder notifications
- New class alerts
- Facility updates and maintenance notices
- Promotional offers for additional services

## What They Do Better Than Us
1. **Utility focus:** App is genuinely useful for daily gym experience
2. **Amenity integration:** Pool, basketball, racquetball all in one app
3. **Scale:** Nationwide access for travelers
4. **Simplicity:** Does a few things really well

## What We Do Better
- More welcoming, community atmosphere
- Better staff-to-member ratios
- Personalized service and attention
- Local events and community connection
- Less corporate, more neighborhood feel

## What We Can Learn
Not every app needs to be gamified or flashy. Solving real problems (like "which classes have spots?") drives consistent usage more than points.

## Member Quotes (from our surveys mentioning LA Fitness)
> "Their app just works - I can see what I need"
> "No frills, but that's fine. It's useful."
> "At least they don't spam me with challenges I don't care about"
