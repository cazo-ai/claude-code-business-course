# Competitor Research: Orangetheory Fitness

## Overview
- **Locations:** 1,500+ studios globally
- **Price Point:** $59-$179/month (premium, higher than FitHub)
- **Brand:** Science-backed, heart-rate based interval training
- **Market Position:** Premium boutique fitness experience

## Their Rewards/App Features

### Orangetheory App
- **Heart rate tracking:** Live during workouts, history after
- **Splat points:** Gamified metric (minutes in orange/red zones)
- **Performance summaries:** Detailed post-workout stats
- **Leaderboards:** Compare with others (optional)
- **Booking system:** Reserve spots, waitlists, cancellations
- **Studio community:** See who's in your class

### What Makes It Work
- **Data-driven:** Concrete metrics members can track
- **Gamification:** Splat points feel like scoring in a game
- **Social accountability:** See your workout buddies
- **Progress visible:** Watch fitness improve over time
- **Tech integration:** Wearable compatibility

## Engagement Tactics
- Post-workout summary emails with stats
- Monthly performance reports
- Milestone celebrations (e.g., 100th class)
- Studio challenges with leaderboards
- Achievement badges and trophies

## What They Do Better Than Us
1. **Measurable progress:** Objective data, not subjective feelings
2. **Gamification done right:** Splat points are addictive
3. **Community visibility:** See friends' activity (with consent)
4. **Premium experience:** App matches the high-end studio vibe

## What We Do Better
- More affordable and accessible
- Flexible workout styles (not just HIIT)
- Less intimidating for beginners
- Variety of class types and equipment access

## Key Takeaway
They've made fitness quantifiable and competitive in a motivating way. Members aren't just "showing up" - they're chasing metrics and beating personal records.

## Member Quotes (from our surveys mentioning OTF)
> "I love seeing my splat points go up"
> "The data makes me competitive with myself"
> "It's addictive trying to beat my last workout"
