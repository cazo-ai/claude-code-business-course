# Member Feedback - March

## Survey Responses (45 responses)

### "How satisfied are you with FitRewards?"
- Very Satisfied: 9%
- Satisfied: 25%
- Neutral: 48%
- Dissatisfied: 14%
- Very Dissatisfied: 4%

### Open-Ended Feedback

**Positive:**
> "Points add up eventually"

**Neutral:**
> "Fine but forgettable"

> "Serviceable"

> "It's whatever"

**Negative:**
> "Boring. No reason to engage."

> "Feels like homework checking in on an app I don't care about"

> "This doesn't make me want to work out more"

> "It's just transactional. No connection to my actual fitness journey."

### In-Gym Comments (from trainer reports)

- Central location: "Member asked 'Why doesn't the app know I'm training for a 5K?'"
- Member suggestion: "What if you matched people with workout styles?"
- Trainer observation: "Members come to FitHub for community but the app is isolating"
- Regular member: "I'm here 4x a week but the app makes me feel like just a number"

### NPS Score This Month
- NPS for FitHub overall: 72 (strong)
- NPS for FitRewards: 27 (weak)

### Competitive Comments
- 2 people mentioned Planet Fitness' judgment-free approach in their app
- 2 people mentioned Peloton's motivational content
- 1 person: "Even Crunch Fitness has better app features"

### One Comment That Stuck
> "Fine but forgettable" - heard this exact phrase twice this month now

## Summary
The gap between FitHub (loved) and FitRewards (meh) is widening. Members want personalization and motivation, not just visit tracking. The "fine but forgettable" theme is emerging.
