# Module 3: Deep Analysis & Synthesis - Exercises

Complete these exercises to master strategic analysis and synthesis skills.

---

## Exercise 1: Root Cause Analysis

**Objective:** Practice identifying root causes vs. symptoms.

**Tasks:**
1. Analyze why FitRewards has low engagement - identify at least 3 root causes with evidence
2. For each root cause, cite specific files and quotes that support it
3. Rank the root causes by impact (which matters most?)

**Success Criteria:**
- Root causes are distinct from symptoms
- Each cause is backed by evidence from multiple sources
- Analysis explains WHY each cause matters

---

## Exercise 2: Cross-Source Synthesis

**Objective:** Practice connecting information from disparate sources.

**Tasks:**
1. Connect member complaints to specific campaigns that may have caused them
2. Link previous manager's observations to actual data in feedback files
3. Connect competitor advantages to FitHub's weaknesses

**Success Criteria:**
- Clear cause-effect relationships identified
- Evidence drawn from at least 2-3 different file types
- Insights reveal hidden connections

---

## Exercise 3: Strategic Recommendation

**Objective:** Practice generating actionable recommendations.

**Tasks:**
Create a `strategic-recommendations.md` file that includes:
1. Problem statement with key metrics
2. Root causes (from Exercise 1)
3. Strategic approach to address them
4. 3-5 specific, actionable recommendations
5. Expected outcomes for each recommendation

**Success Criteria:**
- Recommendations are specific and actionable
- Each is tied to a root cause
- Business impact is clear
- Recommendations are feasible given FitHub's constraints

---

## Exercise 4: Competitive Strategy

**Objective:** Practice competitive analysis and positioning.

**Tasks:**
1. Analyze FitHub's competitive advantages in rewards programs
2. Identify FitHub's competitive disadvantages
3. Recommend 2-3 features FitHub should copy from competitors
4. Recommend 1-2 things FitHub should do differently to stand out

**Success Criteria:**
- Analysis compares FitHub to at least 3 competitors
- Recommendations consider FitHub's Southwest market and brand
- Strategy is differentiated, not just copying

---

## Exercise 5: What Worked / What Didn't

**Objective:** Practice learning from past performance.

**Tasks:**
1. Identify the 2 most successful campaigns and explain why they worked
2. Identify the 2 least successful campaigns and explain why they failed
3. Extract 3-5 principles for future campaigns based on this analysis

**Success Criteria:**
- Success/failure factors are specific and supported
- Principles are actionable for future work
- Analysis goes beyond obvious observations

---

## Exercise 6: Member Segmentation

**Objective:** Practice identifying patterns in customer data.

**Tasks:**
1. Based on feedback files, identify at least 3 different member segments
2. For each segment, describe their unique needs and pain points
3. Recommend specific engagement strategies for each segment

**Success Criteria:**
- Segments are distinct and meaningful
- Evidence from feedback files supports each segment
- Strategies are tailored to each segment's needs

---

## Exercise 7: Data-Driven Business Case

**Objective:** Practice building executive-level business arguments.

**Tasks:**
Create a `business-case.md` that answers: Should FitHub invest in improving FitRewards or shut it down?

Include:
1. Current state metrics
2. Cost of improvement vs. cost of shutdown
3. Risk analysis of both options
4. ROI projections for improvement
5. Clear recommendation with rationale

**Success Criteria:**
- Business case uses actual data from files
- Financial thinking is sound
- Risks are honestly assessed
- Recommendation is defensible

---

## Bonus Challenge

**Executive Presentation:**

Create a 5-minute presentation outline called `exec-presentation.md` that:
- Opens with a compelling hook
- States the problem with key metrics
- Presents 3 root causes
- Offers strategic solution
- Makes specific asks of leadership
- Ends with timeline and success metrics

This should be presentation-ready for FitHub executives.

- [ ] Synthesize information from multiple sources
- [ ] Create strategic recommendations
